The Bishop's Role

The bishop creates, reviews and updates the scriptorium's agreements with sources and libraries, as well as the scriptorium's basic resources. A bit of a micromanager, the bishop also views statistics on the performance of the scriptorium, and the current states of workers.

The Bishop's Behaviors

  • The bishop views and updates the resources of the scriptorium.
  • The bishop creates, views, or updates source agreements.
  • The bishop creates, views, or updates customer agreements.
  • The bishop views the current status of the abbot, the prior, and all provisioners, scribes, archivists, and librarians.
  • The bishop starts the scriptorium by starting up an abbot and a prior.
  • The bishop gracefully stops the scriptorium by gracefully stopping the abbot, the prior, and then all provisioners, scribes, archivists, and librarians.
  • The bishop views aggregate statistics for the scriptorium.
  • The bishop views clears notifications of errors in source agreements, customer agreements, pile tasks, and document tasks.
  • The bishop views and exports documents.
