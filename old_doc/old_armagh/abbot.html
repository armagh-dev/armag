The Abbot's Role

The abbot oversees the operations of the scriptorium. He ensures that there are enough provisioners, scribes and librarians in the abbey to get the work done. Our abbot has a very light touch, though, so he doesn't actually assign tasks to individual workers. Instead, as workers do their jobs, they will post, claim, update and close tasks.

The Abbot's Behaviors

  • The abbot periodically adjusts the number of workers to ensure that:
    • the number of provisioners is appropriate for the current work load, based on the scriptorium's source agreements and the available compute and network resources.
    • the number of scribes is appropriate for the current work load, based on the current unclaimed document tasks and the available compute and I/O resources.
    • the number of librarians is appropriate for the current work load, based on the current number of unclaimed pile tasks, the scriptorium's customer agreements, and the available compute and I/O resources.
    • the number of archivists is appropriate for the current work load, based on the current number of unarchived pile tasks and the available compute and I/O resources.
  • The abbot periodically verifies the health of the prior and all provisioners, scribes, and librarians and, if they are unresponsive, will remove them from service and put their tasks back into the task pool.
  • The abbot notifies the administrator if he is unable to carry out these functions.
  • The abbot shuts itself down if it detects another abbot with a higher process ID.
