The Librarian's Role

The job of the librarian is to collect, package and deliver completed transciptions for customers of the scriptorium. The librarian guarantees that documents are delivered to customers in order of their publication time.

The Librarian's Behaviors

  • The librarian monitors for unclaimed delivery pile tasks and claims the oldest one.1
  • After successfully claiming one, the librarian retrieves the appropriate transcribed documents, packages them as is appropriate for the customer according to the customer agreement, ensures that all older piles have been delivered, and delivers them.
  • If the librarian encounters errors in collecting, packaging, or delivering data, it records error detail in the pile task.

1 The librarian must operate on piles instead of individual documents to ensure that data is delivered to customers in time order.
